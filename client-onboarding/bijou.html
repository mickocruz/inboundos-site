02 / The install

5 core agents, built around your real workflow.

Inquiry, design, strike-off, production, delivery. Plus your outbound.

AGENT 01
CLIO
the intake agent

Trigger: a client submits your project form (or emails sales).

What she does: reads the submission and builds a complete project brief. Client, sector, specs, artwork status, timeline, budget signals. Routes it to your PM with everything organized, and logs the lead into your pipeline.

What dies: owner triage. Projects start themselves.

AGENT 02
HERMES
email triage + sales pipeline

Trigger: every email that hits your inbox, every new lead, every stalled conversation.

What he does: triages your entire inbox. Every email classified (lead, client, vendor, junk), prioritized, routed to the right person with a drafted reply waiting. Leads scored hot, warm, or cold. Follow-up sequences run automatically.

What dies: you and David as the human inbox routers. Dropped follow-ups.

AGENT 03
IRIS
the project status agent

Trigger: project stage changes, plus a weekly schedule.

What she does: sends designers automatic weekly status updates in your voice, and chases strike-off approvals when a sample sits unsigned. Tracks every open approval.

What dies: "any update?" emails. Stalled strike-offs delaying production.

AGENT 04
ARGUS
the spec-check agent

Trigger: a client uploads artwork.

What he does: checks every file against your printing requirements. File type, 100dpi at print size, 2″ bleed, Pantone reference. Emails the client a clear fix-list before your design team ever opens the file.

What dies: back-and-forth on bad files. Design hours burned on pre-flight checks.

AGENT 05
ARES
the outbound agent

Trigger: your cold email campaigns, daily.

What he does: personalized cold emails to designers, architects, and procurement contacts, sent in sequences with reply detection. Replies hand off to Hermes. For cold calls, he preps researched call sheets so every dial starts with context.

What dies: manual list grinding. Cold leads going dark after one touch. Calling blind.
